Niagara Falls

Geographical Location

North America

Niagara Falls is located on the border between Canada and the United States. Connecting Lake Erie and Lake Ontario, Niagara Falls has the highest flow rate of any waterfall in North America.

Niagara Falls draws in thousands of visitors every year, and for good reason. As such, there are numerous things that you can see while you visit the falls, and it is very easy to make a full trip out of seeing this wonderful sight.

Grand Canyon

Geographical Location

North America

The Grand Canyon is located in Arizona, United States. It is a 277-mile-long canyon that was carved out by the Colorado River over millions of years.

The Grand Canyon is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the United States. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is visited by millions of people every year.

Great Wall of China

Geographical Location

Asia

The Great Wall of China is located in China. It is a series of fortifications that were built to protect the Chinese Empire from invaders. It is the longest wall in the world, stretching over 13,000 miles.

The Great Wall of China is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is visited by millions of people every year.
